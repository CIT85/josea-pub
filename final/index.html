Mountains




About

Mountains are large landforms that rise above the surrounding terrain. They form through tectonic uplift, volcanic activity, or the folding and faulting of the Earth's crust, a process that can take millions of years.

Mountains influence climate and weather patterns, supply freshwater from snow and glacier melt, and support unique alpine ecosystems.


They are also important for recreation (hiking, climbing, skiing), local economies, and cultural or spiritual traditions.

Typical features: peaks, ridges, valleys, and alpine zones.

How they affect people:sources of water, tourism, and sometimes natural hazards like avalanches and landslides.

Conservation: many mountain habitats are sensitive to climate change and human impact.
