Deserts

About

Deserts are arid regions that receive very little precipitation, typically less than 250 millimeters (10 inches) annually. They can be hot, like the Sahara Desert, or cold, like the Gobi Desert. Deserts are characterized by extreme temperatures, sparse vegetation, and unique adaptations of plants and animals to survive in harsh conditions.


Sahara Desert




Fun Fact:The Sahara has been a significant cultural and historical region, influencing trade routes and the movement of peoples throughout history. It has also been the subject of various studies related to climate change and human adaptation to extreme environments.
